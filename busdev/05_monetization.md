# Monetization Strategy

As a solo developer or small team, the monetization approach must be realistic, sustainable, and aligned with user value, particularly considering the costs of future cloud infrastructure.

## 1. Affiliate Marketing (Primary Driver)
- **Concept:** Recommend specific, high-quality spare parts (e.g., Motul oil, Daytona brake pads, genuine V-Belts) within the service result page.
- **Execution:** Use affiliate links for major Indonesian e-commerce platforms (Tokopedia, Shopee). 
- **Why it works:** Low friction, provides massive value (directing users away from expensive official dealers to affordable third-party options online), and scales infinitely without requiring inventory. This is the primary revenue stream intended to subsidize free server usage.

## 2. Premium / "Pro" Subscription
- **Concept:** The core app remains free for 1 vehicle.
- **Execution:** Introduce a low-cost, sustainable annual subscription (e.g., Rp 20.000/year) to cover cloud sync server costs, moving away from unsustainable one-time fees. 
- **Pro Features:** 
  - Track unlimited motorcycles.
  - Export service history to PDF.
  - Real-time cloud backup.

## 3. Workshop Lead Generation (Future)
- **Concept:** Connect users needing major services with vetted local independent mechanics.
- **Execution:** Workshops pay a small monthly listing fee or a per-lead fee to appear as a "Recommended Garage" when a user's odometer hits a major milestone.

## 4. What to Avoid
- **No intrusive banner ads:** They degrade the premium UX/UI.
- **No selling identifiable user data:** Keep the focus on privacy and utility.
